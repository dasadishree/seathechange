Saving Sea Turtles

Sea turtles have existed for over 100 million years, but today they face numerous threats that put their survival at risk. From plastic pollution to habitat destruction, these ancient mariners need our help more than ever.

The main threats to sea turtle populations include plastic pollution mistaken for food, loss of nesting beaches due to development, climate change affecting egg incubation, and accidental capture in fishing gear. These challenges have led to declining populations across all sea turtle species.

SEAA works tirelessly to protect sea turtles through beach monitoring during nesting season, education programs for local communities, rescue and rehabilitation of injured turtles, and advocacy for turtle-safe fishing practices. Our dedicated team and volunteers make a real difference in sea turtle conservation.

Join our efforts by volunteering for beach cleanups, supporting our rehabilitation programs, or spreading awareness about sea turtle conservation. Every action counts in protecting these magnificent creatures for future generations.
